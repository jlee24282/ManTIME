Minister of Maghreb Affairs, African Union and the Arab League Abdelkader Messahel hailed on <TIMEX3 tid="t1" type="DATE" value="2017-01-28">Saturday</TIMEX3>, in Addis Ababa (Ethiopia), the election of Fatima Zahra Karadja as a member of the Panel of Eminent Persons of the African Peer Review Mechanism (APRM), APS reported. 
Messahel welcomed, in a statement to the press on the sidelines of the 26th Forum of the Heads of State and Government of the APRM, the election of Fatima Zahra Karadja as a member of the Panel of Eminent Persons of the African Peer Review Mechanism (APRM), adding that this is a pride for the country and for the Algerian woman. 
Messahel also recalled Chafika Bensaoula’s election as a judge at the African Court on Human and Peoples’ Rights. 
 
The official reiterated that these two significant events have paid tribute to Algerian woman, highlighting that President of the Republic has always promoted Algerian woman position; therefore, she is represented at high international bodies.
