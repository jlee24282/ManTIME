OWENSBORO, Ky. (<TIMEX3 tid="t1" type="DATE" value="2017-01-29">1/29/17</TIMEX3>) — After a body was discovered in a backyard in Owensboro, two people, including the victim's mother, have been charged in connection to the death. 
 
According to the Owensboro Police Department, <TIMEX3 tid="t3" type="TIME" value="2017-01-29T15:15" mod="APPROX">around 3:15 p.m.</TIMEX3>, <TIMEX3 tid="t2" type="DATE" value="2017-01-28">yesterday</TIMEX3>, officers responded to 802 Breckenridge Street to investigate a body found in the back yard by the property owner. The identity of the deceased was Jose’ Breydi Gomez, 30, of the 700 block of Breckenridge Street. It is believed Gomez had been deceased for <TIMEX3 tid="t4" type="DURATION" value="PXD">several days</TIMEX3>. 
 
The investigation led detectives to believe Gomez was struck by a piece of wood, causing his death. 
 
The victim’s mother, Yolanda Gomez, 52, of Owensboro, and an acquaintance, Salvador Avila-Navarrete, 46, also of Owensboro, were arrested and charged in connection to the death. 
 
Avila-Navarrete was charged with first degree manslaughter and tampering with physical evidence. Yolanda Gomez was charged with tampering with physical evidence. 
 
Both were lodged in the Daviess County Detention Center. 
 
Autopsy results are pending. This investigation is ongoing.
