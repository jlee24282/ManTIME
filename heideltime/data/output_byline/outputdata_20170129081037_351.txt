Born in Shickshinny, he was the son of the late Merthen and Irene (Thompson) Edwards. Jim, as he was affectionately known, had worked as a machinist for the former American Car & Foundry, Berwick PA, also for the Navy Ship Parts Control Center, Mechanicsburg for <TIMEX3 tid="t6" type="DURATION" value="P19Y">19 years</TIMEX3> before retiring. Jim proudly served his country in the U.S. Army during WWII, fighting in the "Battle of the Bulge" and received 2 Bronze Stars. He was also recognized as the oldest living WWII veteran at Masonic Village, and was a recipient of the "Quilt of Valor." He was a 32nd degree mason Valley of Bloomsburg Lodge Council, Scottish Rite of Bloomsburg, and a member of the Consistory Valley of Bloomsburg. He was also a member of the D.A.V. of Camp Hill. Jim attended the John S. Sell Memorial Chapel at Masonic Village, along with his wife "Vi." Jim's other interests included fishing, painting, and he was a member of the Art Club of Masonic Village. 
 
Surviving in addition to his wife Vi is a son James husband of Susan Edwards, Pottsville; a daughter Ann Beagle, Fredericksburg, VA; a step-daughter Diane wife of Thomas Nowicki, Woodbury, CT; five grandchildren; several great-grandchildren; and two sisters, Irene Davis, Mountain Top, PA and Gwen Williams, Mount Joy. In addition to his first wife and parents, he was preceded in death by a grandson.
