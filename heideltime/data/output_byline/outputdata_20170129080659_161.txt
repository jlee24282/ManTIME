The home team used three relay team victories to win the Montrose Invitational girls swim meet on <TIMEX3 tid="t8" type="DATE" value="2017-01-28">Saturday</TIMEX3>. 
 
The Montrose team of Tatum McHugh, Alli Fields, Gabrielle Chandler and Maggie McHugh won the 400-yard freestyle relay, and Isabella Benitez, Megan Nichols, Taylor Ullmann and Fields won the 200 freestyle relay. Tatum and Maggie McHugh, Chandler and Benitez made up the winning 200 medley relay team. 
 
The Indians won the meet with 164 points, with Fruita Monument in second with 125 and Grand Junction’s 117 points good for third. 
 
Grand Junction’s Delaney Kidd was a double winner, winning the 100 butterfly and the 100 breaststroke. 
 
Her teammate, Sarah Griffin, was second in the 100 butterfly. 
 
Delta’s Kendra Hillman was second in the 100 breaststroke. 
 
Fields was also a double winner, taking the 500 and 200 freestyle races for the Indians. 
 
Maggie McHugh was the 100 freestyle winner, and Tatum McHugh won the 100 backstroke for Montrose. 
 
Fruita Monument’s Natalie McConnell was second in the 100 and 200 freestyle. Kenley Kohls won the 1-meter diving event for the Wildcats. 
 
Grand Junction swept the top two places in the 500 freestyle with Madeline Cawood winning the race and Clarissa Berry taking second. 
 
Boys Basketball 
 
Central held off a hot-shooting squad from Glenwood Springs to pick up a 64-62 nonleague win in Glenwood Springs. 
 
The Warriors (13-4) looked to be in good shape with a 29-20 lead at the half, but Glenwood’s hot shooting in <TIMEX3 tid="t9" type="DATE" value="2016-Q3">the third quarter</TIMEX3> gave the Demons the lead at 46-45 going into the final period. 
 
Glenwood (10-8) had seven 3-pointers in the game and had a chance to tie late, but a steal by Central’s Julio Galindo sealed the win for the Warriors.

Wrestling 
 
Central had a strong showing at the Thornton Invitational, finishing second. 
 
The Warriors (195 points) were second to Alamosa (225 1/2 in the 21-team tournament 225 1/2 to 195. 
 
Central had six wrestlers in the finals, with three winning titles. 
 
Riley Garner-Orr had two pins and two major decisions to win at 138. E.J. Barrera got a pin in the 170-pound title match, and Weston Holdren pinned his way to a title at 182. 
 
Tanner Hickey (160), Robert Shenk (195) and Andrew Bench (132) each took second.
