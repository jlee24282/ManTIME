HINSDALE, Mass. — Earl Edward Carmel Jr., 91, of Nashua, N.H., formerly of Hinsdale, Mass., died <TIMEX3 tid="t6" type="DATE" value="2017-01-24">Tuesday, January 24, 2017</TIMEX3>, at Southern New Hampshire Medical Center in Nashua.  
 

Born in Pittsfield on <TIMEX3 tid="t10" type="DATE" value="1925-08-17">August 17, 1925</TIMEX3>, the son of Earl E. Carmel, Sr. and Ruth Sherman Carmel, he attended schools in Hinsdale and graduated from Pittsfield High School. He was a World War II veteran, where is was involved in the European Theater and was Honorably discharged.  
 

A mink rancher by profession, Mr. Carmel owned and operated Carmel's Mink Ranch in Hinsdale for <TIMEX3 tid="t13" type="DURATION" value="P65Y">65 years</TIMEX3>. He mentored many young people at the ranch helping then with a good foundation with good work ethics . He was also active on both national and state-wide ranching committees. At one time, Mr. Carmel worked for General Electric Company, and had been in business with his brother in the operation of a service station in Hinsdale.  
 

Mr. Carmel was Chairman of the Hinsdale Planning Board for <TIMEX3 tid="t14" type="DURATION" value="PXY">several years</TIMEX3>. He enjoyed traveling and trapping.

Carmel, Ivy L. Carmel, and  Nichole Kennedy.
