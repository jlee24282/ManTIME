A Dubuque museum will host a special poster exhibit in <TIMEX3 tid="t1" type="DATE" value="2017-02">February</TIMEX3> in recognition of Black History Month. 
 
The National Mississippi River Museum & Aquarium will host “A Place for All People: Introducing the National Museum of African American History and Culture” beginning <TIMEX3 tid="t3" type="DATE" value="2017-02-01">Wednesday, Feb. 1</TIMEX3>. The exhibit will be on display from <TIMEX3 tid="t8" type="TIME" value="2017-02-01T10:00">10 a.m.</TIMEX3> to <TIMEX3 tid="t10" type="TIME" value="2017-01-31T17:00">5 p.m. Tuesday</TIMEX3> through <TIMEX3 tid="t7" type="DATE" value="2017-01-29">Sunday</TIMEX3> through <TIMEX3 tid="t5" type="DATE" value="2017-02-28">Feb. 28</TIMEX3>. 
 
The poster exhibit focuses on “key artifacts” housed in the Smithsonian’s new National Museum of African American History and Culture in Washington, D.C. The Dubuque museum is affiliated with the Smithsonian. 
 
Among the artifacts featured at the new museum are child-size shackles of a slave and clothing worn by Carolotta Walls, one of the first black students at Little Rock Central High School. 
 
The exhibition “presents a living history that reflects challenge, triumph, faith and hope,” according to a press release.
