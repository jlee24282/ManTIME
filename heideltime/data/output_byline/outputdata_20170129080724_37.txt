KATHMANDU: Prime Minister Pushpa Kamal Dahal laid a wreath at the Martyrs’ Monument at Martyrs’ Memorial Park in Lainchaur <TIMEX3 tid="t8" type="DATE" value="2017-01-29">today</TIMEX3> on the occasion of the Martyrs’ Day. 
 
PM Dahal paid tributes to the martyrs on the final day of the Martyrs’ Week by laying the wreath. 
 
On the occasion, <TIMEX3 tid="t10" type="DURATION" value="PT1M">a minute</TIMEX3> of silence was observed in the honour of the martyrs. 
  
Prime Minister Pushpa Kamal Dahal pays his tributes to the martyrs on the occasion of the Martyrs’ Day at the Martyrs’ Memorial Park in Lainchaur, on <TIMEX3 tid="t16" type="DATE" value="2017-01-29">Sunday, January 29, 2017</TIMEX3>. Photo Courtesy: PM’s Secretariat 
  
During the ceremony, Speaker Onsari Gharti Magar, ministers, Chief Secretary Dr Som Lal Subedi, Chief of Army Staff and Inspector General of Police among top government officials also offered garlands on the martyrs’ monument. 
  
High officials line up to pay their tributes to the martyrs on the occasion of the Martyrs’ Day at the Martyrs’ Memorial Park in Lainchaur, on <TIMEX3 tid="t23" type="DATE" value="2017-01-29">Sunday, January 29, 2017</TIMEX3>. Photo Courtesy: PM’s Secretariat 
  
A rally organised by the Kathmandu Metropolitan City (KMC) that started from the Shanti Batika in Ratna Park had converged into a memorial ceremony at Martyrs’ Park in Lainchaur. 
 
<TIMEX3 tid="t25" type="SET" value="P1Y" quant="EVERY">Every year</TIMEX3>, Magh 16 of the Nepali year is celebrated as the Martyrs’ Day to commemorate the known and unknown martyrs.
