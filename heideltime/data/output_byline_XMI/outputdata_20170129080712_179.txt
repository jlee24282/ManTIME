And true to form, when it comes to social media in law enforcement, the SMILE pioneers at Toronto Police Service, are taking more than a few arrows in the back. &#10; For the TPS, it didn’t start with G20.  &#10;Wind the clock back to May when 18 y.o. The cops are unable to comment by law due to an independent investigation, but thousands of members of the court of public opinion say the cops killed him. Mills has spent years building relationships through social media with the same citizens who are now joining forces against the TPS. Dealing with crisis communications needs to be part of your social media strategy. TPS could have been even better prepared in the online world, but the fact that they produced a social media guide ahead of time and actively monitored social networks with a strategy in place, was remarkable. Engagement is king. TPS was ready to communicate and did so with anyone and everyone who engaged them. If instead, they used Twitter, Facebook, YouTube and the rest only to distribute and not receive, they would have missed a huge opportunity.  Use social media for internal communication.
