They used that area, combined it with an assumption that every inch of that area is planted with bluegrass, and came up with a formula that spits out enough water to take care of all of that. &#10; &#10;I think its the core of what the water budget is all about, City Manager Roy Otto said, referencing the personalized water budget. &#10; &#10;Inside, the city will budget 55 gallons per person per day, with the assumption that there are three people in every household. (Call the city if you have a larger family). &#10; &#10;City officials say the budget should provide more than enough water for most residents. In testing the program with 16,700 households, the city found 83 percent of folks stayed within budget. &#10; &#10;If you dont, well, youll get charged more. This years base rate is $3.97 per 1,000 gallons, which is actually cheaper than what folks paid last year. &#10; &#10;If you use up to 130 percent more than your budget, youll pay $4.85 per gallon for anything over your budget. The rates go up to $6.62 and then $8.82 per 1,000 gallons from there. Just 4 percent of the pilot group of 16,700 households went that far over their budget. &#10; &#10;We think weve developed a program that will reward efficiency, and yet it will also provide an incentive for people who are over-watering to cut back, Evans said. &#10; &#10;For now, the changes wont apply to businesses or other commercial property. Thats a little more tricky. &#10; &#10;A typical, tiered-rate structure would have been easier. That would involve one rate for a certain amount of water, and higher rates for anything above that. &#10; &#10;Fort Collins has one, and such a structure can punish homeowners with a larger lot even if those homeowners practice water conservation. &#10; &#10;Without that work done by the staff, we would have arbitrarily penalized people for being efficient with their water just for having a larger lot, Otto said. &#10; &#10;City officials spent so much time studying the outdoor water use because they say its the area with the most potential for savings. In some months  particularly April and October, when people run their sprinklers more than necessary  the city as a whole could cut water usage by half or more, according to city data. &#10; &#10;As Greeley grows toward 250,000 people by 2060, getting folks in the habit of thinking about water conservation will be key, Evans said. &#10; &#10;Theres a finite supply of water in Colorado, Evans said.
