&quot;Waltz&quot; was the big star, big deal finale for the Band, the American rock group that featured Robbie Robertson, Rick Danko, Levon Helm, Garth Hudson and Richard Manuel. The concert, advertised as the guys' farewell concert appearance, was held in San Francisco on Thanksgiving Day, Nov. 25, 1976. The party attracted stars like Muddy Waters, Bob Dylan, Neil Diamond, Joni Mitchell, Eric Clapton and Van Morrison. Ringo Starr, Paul Butterfield, Ronnie Wood, Neil Young and Dr. John also stopped by. &#10; &#10;The show lives on, with an assist from Martin Scorsese. The film director had cameras inside San Fran's Winterland Ballroom and edited songs and interviews into 1978's &quot;The Last Waltz&quot; movie, acclaimed as one of the greatest concert films ever made. &#10; &#10;Was and his pals have said hello to the classic material and have brought back the live show. &quot;Last Waltz New Orleans&quot; was a monster hit at the city's Jazz Fest in 2016 and the &quot;Waltz&quot; ensemble later decided to take the music and run for an 11-stop tour that includes Albany, Atlanta, Boston, Philadelphia and Washington. &#10; &#10;The Albany show will be held Thursday, Feb. 2 at the Palace Theatre. It could have been a train wreck or it could have been something great, but we clicked from the first rehearsal. Everyone playing in the band was really knocked out. that doesn't always happen. Usually, it doesn't. This is a serious band and it's a great honor to play with these guys.&quot; &#10; &#10; &#10; &#10;Was, 64, is only talking about &quot;Last Waltz&quot; during this round of interviews - so no questions about the Rolling Stones, no questions about jazz.
