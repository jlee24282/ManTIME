Maybe in the state tournament it’s OK to run – I still don’t think it’s OK to run – but in these small tournaments, I think it’s awesome that these high-caliber guys wrestle each other.” &#10; &#10;The capacity crowd at Bedford High School seemed to think so, too. The match was held until the very end of the tournament and few fans left early. The crowd was into the bout from the start, which Murin noticed, even his hyper-focused state. &#10; &#10;“Every time someone took a shot, the fans went crazy, which is awesome for the sport of wrestling,” he said. “It was an exciting match, and it was nice having that commentary in the back.” &#10; &#10;After a scoreless first period, Murin escaped in the second, and after letting one potential takedown slip away, he scored with about 15 seconds remaining for a 3-0 lead. &#10; &#10;Murin said he relied on his previous matches – including a summer victory over Courtney in Orlando, Florida – to know how to score. &#10; &#10;“I took him down with a low double twice – I took him down here last year and took him down at Disney,” Murin said. “I knew if I hit it nice, it was going to work.” &#10; &#10;Courtney escaped in the third, but couldn’t score a tying takedown.
